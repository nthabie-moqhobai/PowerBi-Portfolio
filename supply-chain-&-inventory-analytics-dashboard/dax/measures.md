# 📊 DAX Measures — Supply Chain & Inventory Analytics Dashboard
This section outlines the key DAX measures used to drive insights across sales performance, inventory efficiency, demand trends, and product prioritization.

---

## 🔷 Core Sales & Inventory Metrics

These measures provide a foundational view of overall business performance.

* **Total Sales** – Aggregated revenue across all transactions
* **Total Units Sold** – Total quantity of products sold
* **Inventory On Hand** – Current stock available across locations

---

## ⏳ Time Intelligence Measures

Designed to evaluate demand trends and performance over time.

* **Sales (Previous Month)** – Total sales for the prior month
* **Sales (Previous Year)** – Total sales for the same period last year
* **Sales MoM % Change** – Month-over-Month growth rate in sales
* **Sales YoY % Change** – Year-over-Year growth rate in sales

---

## 📦 Inventory Health & Efficiency

These measures assess how effectively inventory is being managed.

* **Average Daily Sales** – Estimated daily demand based on historical sales
* **Inventory Turnover** – Frequency at which inventory is sold and replenished
* **Days of Inventory Coverage** – Number of days current stock can sustain demand
* **Inventory–Sales Gap** – Difference between available stock and demand levels
* **Stockout Risk Indicator** – Flag identifying potential stock shortages

---

## 📊 Pareto (ABC) Analysis Measures

Used to prioritize products based on their contribution to total sales.

* **Sales Rank** – Product ranking based on revenue contribution
* **Sales Contribution %** – Individual product’s share of total sales
* **Cumulative Sales** – Running total of sales across ranked products
* **Cumulative Contribution %** – Accumulated percentage of total sales
* **ABC Classification** – Categorization into A, B, or C tiers based on contribution

---

## 📌 Key Insight

These measures work together to transform raw data into actionable insights, enabling:

* Proactive inventory planning
* Identification of demand trends
* Prioritization of high-impact products
* Early detection of stock risks
